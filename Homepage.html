Local-first workspace

Single-file notes with a frosted, offline-first experience.

NoteFlow ships as a single HTML file that runs fully in your browser. Create nested pages, paste images, use templates and slash-commands, and export a JSON backup whenever you like. No accounts or background syncs — your data stays with you unless you choose otherwise.

Focused on control

Designed to keep your notes close

Local-first by design: your data never leaves your machine unless you export it. NoteFlow brings a calm interface, glass panels, and dependable structure for everyday work.

Rich editor & content

Headings, lists, code blocks, callouts, tables, and inline embeds. Paste images directly — files are embedded as base64 if needed.

Pages, templates, and organization

Deeply nested pages via a simple separator, reusable templates (meeting notes, journals, plans), pin and star important pages.

Fast themes & focus

Multiple theme presets, per-page theming, and a focus mode to remove distractions and boost productivity.

Export, history & backups

One-click JSON export/import, 50-state undo/redo per page, and an optional Google Drive backup that uses your own credentials.

Why local-first

Stay fast, private, and portable

NoteFlow keeps essentials inside one HTML file so you can move, rename, and back up without worrying about accounts or outages.

Private by default

No background syncs, no accounts—just your notes stored in browser storage.

Portable everywhere

Rename it to index.html and carry the full experience on a USB or cloud folder.

Backups you control

Export to JSON whenever you want; keep your own cadence and storage location.

Fast setup

Start in under a minute

NoteFlow is intentionally simple to start. Download, open, and save locally. Backups are yours to control.

1

Open the HTML

Download or rename it to index.html. Double-click to open in any modern browser.

2

Build your space

Create nested pages, add tasks, drop images, and reuse templates for recurring notes.

3

Save and back up

Work saves to localStorage. Export everything as JSON or connect your own Drive keys if desired.
